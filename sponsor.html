Honorable minister Nkeiruka Onyejeocha

She is a Nigerian politician and a ranking member of the federal house of representatives .

Biography

She was born on November 23rd, 1969 in Isuochi, Umunneochi L.G.A, Abia State, Nigeria.

She was born into the family of late Eze Bob Ogbonna. She had a primary school education at Isuochi central primary school and Ovim girls school, Isuochi. She graduated from the university of Nigeria, Nsukka with a diploma of social works/community development in 1998 and a bachelor of arts degree in 1993.

Political Career

Onyejeocha worked in the protocol office of the governor of Osun state during her one-year stay at the NYSC in 1993. She is now the present minister of labour in Abuja, Nigeria

Key Achievements

Her achievements are as follows: 1. Legislative reforms 2. Women empowerment 3. Constituency development 4. Awards and recognition 5. Leadership roles 6. Community development 7. Advocacy

These acheivements showcase her commitment to the public service, leadership and community development.

Senator Ireti Kingibe

A prominent Nigerian politician and former First Lady of Nigeria.

Biography

Ireti Kingibe is a prominent Nigerian politician and former First Lady of Nigeria. She has been involved in Nigerian politics for over two decades, serving in various capacities at the state and federal levels.

Ireti Kingibe was born in Lagos, Nigeria, and studied at the University of Ibadan, where she obtained a degree in Political Science. She later went on to earn a master's degree in Public Administration from the University of Lagos.

Political Career

Ireti Kingibe's political career began in the early 1990s when she was appointed as a commissioner in the Lagos State Government. She later served as the First Lady of Nigeria from 1999 to 2007, during the presidency of her husband, Baba Gana Kingibe.

In 2011, Ireti Kingibe was elected to the Nigerian Senate, representing the Federal Capital Territory (FCT) Abuja. She has since been re-elected to the Senate and has served on several committees, including the Senate Committee on Foreign Affairs and the Senate Committee on Women Affairs.

Key Achievements

During her time as First Lady, Ireti Kingibe was known for her advocacy work on issues such as women's empowerment, child welfare, and HIV/AIDS awareness. She also founded the Ireti Kingibe Foundation, which focuses on supporting women and children in Nigeria.

As a Senator, Ireti Kingibe has been a vocal advocate for gender equality and has worked to pass legislation that promotes the rights of women and children in Nigeria. She has also been recognized for her efforts to improve the lives of her constituents in the FCT.
